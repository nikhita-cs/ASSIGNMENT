##  Brief About the Report  

This report explains the fundamental concepts of Bias and Variance in Machine Learning and their impact on model performance.  

It discusses underfitting, overfitting, and the Bias–Variance Tradeoff, which plays a crucial role in selecting the optimal model complexity.  

The report includes mathematical explanations, graphical interpretations, practical control techniques, and real-world applications. The goal is to understand how to build models that generalize well to unseen data by maintaining a balance between bias and variance.

##  Key Concepts Covered  

- Bias (High and Low)  
- Variance (High and Low)  
- Underfitting  
- Overfitting  
- Bias–Variance Tradeoff  
- Total Error Formula  

---

##  Conclusion  

The ideal machine learning model should achieve low bias and low variance, ensuring accurate predictions and strong generalization performance.
